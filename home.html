🏠 Rainy-day and low-energy backups

Fun without loading the car.

Quick activities that work across ages 3–10, with little setup and mostly ordinary household supplies.

🎨 Tiny art studio

Set out paper, markers, tape, stickers, and recycled boxes. Challenge: create a new animal, amusement park, or superhero headquarters.

🏅 Living-room Olympics

Paper-plate discus, sock toss, hallway long jump, cup stacking, freeze dance, and a stuffed-animal relay.

🔎 Color scavenger hunt

Find one safe item in every rainbow color. Older kids photograph or write clues; younger kids collect items in a basket.

🍿 Movie theater day

Make tickets, assign jobs, dim the lights, serve popcorn in paper bags, and let each child vote from the movie list below.

🧪 Kitchen science

Try baking-soda fizz cups, sink-or-float predictions, frozen-toy rescue, or color mixing with water and food coloring.

🧁 Decorate-your-own snack

Use cookies, waffles, yogurt bowls, mini pizzas, or cupcakes. Put toppings in small cups so everyone can build their own.

📖 Cousin storybook

Each child creates one page about the visit. Staple the pages into a keepsake book before they leave.

🧺 Picnic switch-up

Eat lunch on a blanket in the living room, backyard, or nearby green space. Add bubbles, chalk, or a portable speaker.

🕵️ Secret mission

Hide five clues around the house leading to a small prize, dessert, or the next movie choice.

Movie night menu

A mix for preschoolers and elementary-age kids.

🌊

Moana / Moana 2

Adventure, music, and broad age appeal.

🧠

Inside Out 1 or 2

Funny, colorful, and good for talking about feelings.

🍄

The Super Mario Bros. Movie

Fast, familiar, and easy for mixed ages.

🐼

Kung Fu Panda

Action, comedy, and a positive message.

🕷️

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Best for the older children; visually exciting.

🐻

Paddington 2

Warm, funny, and genuinely enjoyable for adults.

🧱

The LEGO Movie

High-energy fun with plenty for different ages.

🎤

Sing / Sing 2

Music-heavy and good for a dance break afterward.

Easy rule: one energetic activity, one quiet activity, one snack, and one movie can fill an entire recovery day.
